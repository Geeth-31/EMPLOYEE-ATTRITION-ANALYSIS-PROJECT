# Dashboard Report

## Project Title

**Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard**

---

## Objective

The objective of this dashboard is to provide an interactive visualization of employee attrition patterns within the organization. It helps identify the major factors influencing employee attrition and supports HR professionals in making data-driven decisions to improve employee retention.

---

## Dashboard Tool

- Tableau Public

---

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard contains interactive KPI cards and visualizations that summarize employee attrition from multiple perspectives.

### KPI Cards

- Total Employees
- Employees Left
- Active Employees
- Attrition Rate

### Visualizations

The dashboard includes the following visualizations:

1. Department-wise Attrition
2. Job Role-wise Attrition
3. Gender-wise Attrition
4. Marital Status-wise Attrition
5. Overtime-wise Attrition
6. Business Travel-wise Attrition
7. Job Satisfaction-wise Attrition
8. Job Level-wise Attrition
9. Environment Satisfaction-wise Attrition
10. Work-Life Balance-wise Attrition

---

## Interactive Filters

The dashboard provides the following interactive filters:

- Department
- Gender
- Job Role

These filters update all visualizations simultaneously, allowing users to explore attrition patterns for different employee groups.

---

## Key Dashboard Insights

The dashboard highlights several important observations:

- Although Research & Development recorded the highest number of employees leaving, the Sales department showed the highest attrition rate (20.63%), making it the highest-risk department.
- Sales Representatives recorded the highest attrition rate (39.76%) among all job roles, indicating that this role faces the greatest employee turnover relative to its size.
- Employees who frequently work overtime show higher attrition.
- Employees with lower monthly income are more likely to leave the organization.
- Younger employees exhibit higher attrition compared to older employees.
- Employees with lower job levels generally experience higher attrition.
- Business travel frequency influences employee turnover.
- Job satisfaction, environment satisfaction, and work-life balance show noticeable relationships with employee attrition.
