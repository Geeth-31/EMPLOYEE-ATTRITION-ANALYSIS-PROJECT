# Business Recommendations

## Objective

Based on the insights obtained from the Employee Attrition Analysis, the following recommendations are proposed to improve employee retention and reduce attrition.

## Recommendations

**1. Focus retention efforts on the Sales department**

The Sales department recorded the highest attrition rate (**20.63%**), making it the highest-risk department. Although Research & Development had more employees leaving overall, this was mainly because it has the largest workforce. Retention strategies should therefore prioritize Sales while continuing to monitor R&D because of its larger employee base.

**2. Prioritize retention for Sales Representatives**

Sales Representatives showed the highest attrition rate (**39.76%**) among all job roles. Conducting role-specific surveys, reviewing workloads, improving incentives, and providing better career growth opportunities could help reduce turnover in this group.

**3. Reduce excessive overtime**

Overtime employees showed **30.53%** attrition compared to just **10.44%** for non-overtime employees — almost 3x higher, and this was one of the strongest patterns in the whole analysis. Monitoring workloads and reducing unnecessary overtime could directly help lower attrition here.

**4. Strengthen career development for younger and lower-level employees**

Employees who left had a median age of **32** vs **36** for employees who stayed, and lower job levels also showed higher attrition since Job Level and Monthly Income are closely linked (correlation of **0.95**). Providing clearer promotion paths and mentorship for younger/lower-level employees could help retain them longer.

**5. Review compensation for lower-income employees**

Employees who left had a median monthly income of **3202**, compared to **5204** for employees who stayed. Regularly reviewing pay for lower-income employees could reduce this gap and improve retention.

**6. Improve work-life balance policies**

Employees with the poorest work-life balance score showed **31.25%** attrition, compared to under 18% for employees with balanced scores — this was one of the biggest gaps found in the whole dataset. Introducing more flexible work policies could directly target this group.

**7. Address job satisfaction and environment satisfaction gaps**

Employees with the lowest job satisfaction score showed **22.84%** attrition vs **11.33%** for the highest score, and lowest environment satisfaction showed **25.35%** attrition, almost double the higher-satisfaction groups. Periodic satisfaction surveys could help catch these issues early before employees decide to leave.

**8. Review business travel requirements**

Employees who travel frequently showed **24.91%** attrition compared to just **8%** for employees with no travel. Reducing unnecessary travel or providing more support for frequent travelers could help here.

**9. Build a more positive work environment overall**

Since environment satisfaction and work-life balance both showed clear links to attrition, encouraging better communication, recognition and supportive leadership could help improve retention across the board, not just for the high risk groups above.

**10. Use dashboards for ongoing monitoring**

The Tableau dashboard built from this analysis can be used going forward to keep tracking these same metrics (overtime, satisfaction, income, department) so that early signs of attrition risk can be caught before they turn into actual employees leaving.

## Expected Outcomes

If these recommendations are followed, the organization can expect:

- Lower attrition, especially among overtime employees and Laboratory Technicians
- Better retention among younger and lower job level employees
- Improved job and environment satisfaction scores
- More balanced workloads and reduced burnout
- Reduced recruitment and training costs from lower turnover

## Conclusion

so overall, attrition in this company isnt caused by one single factor but a mix of overtime, income, job level, satisfaction and work-life balance issues, with overtime and work-life balance showing the biggest gaps in the analysis. focusing on these areas first should give the best results for reducing attrition going forward.
